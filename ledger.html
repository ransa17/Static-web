THE QUIET WISDOM

To our children, our grandchildren, and all who will carry this name after we are gone—

When I was a young boy in Pilani, my father, Rameshwardasji, sat with me under the neem tree in our courtyard. The sun was setting over the Arvallis, painting the sands in gold. He took my small hand and placed it on his chest.

"Do you feel this heartbeat, beta?" he asked.

I nodded.

"This is not just my heart. This is the heartbeat of generations. The Mandelia name is not a label—it is a trust. Hold it carefully, for you are merely its custodian for your time."

I have never forgotten that evening. And now, as I write this from the quiet of my study, I pass that same truth to you.

"Wealth is like the sands of the Arvallis—it shifts with the wind."

I have seen empires rise in the morning and vanish by dusk. I have seen our name etched in the stone of Pilani and heard it in the busy markets of Calcutta. I learned that while the Hisab (accounting) is done in books, the Virasat (legacy) is built on character.

We are Mandelias. We do not just trade, we build. We build schools so the mind can grow; we build mandirs so the soul can find rest; and we build businesses so the family can endure.

"The root of a banyan tree stays alive even when the branches are pruned."

There was a time when our seat at the table was the highest. If time has taken its toll, remember: the root stays alive. My life was a bridge between the glory of our forefathers and the potential of your future.

I leave you not just with assets, but with an instruction:

Build quietly. Do not chase the noise of the world. Invest with Sanyam (restraint) and Maryada (dignity). Whether you are in the heart of Rajasthan or the far corners of the world, never let the ink on your Bahi-Khata dry, and never let the flame of our values flicker.

To our daughters, to our granddaughters—you carry this name with the same weight, the same honor, the same responsibility as any son. In the quiet strength of our women, we have seen the true spine of this family. The blessings fall upon you equally. You are not just the keepers of our traditions—you are the weavers of its future.

"The Mandelia name is a trust. I have held it for my time; now you must hold it for yours."

Rebuild not just the fortune, but the Dharma that created it.

"The sun always returns to the desert. So shall we."

— Late Shri Sanwalram Rameshwardas Mandelia

1929–2012

Pilani | Calcutta, Winter of 2010
